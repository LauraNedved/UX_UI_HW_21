Case Study 1 - NASA UI Website Redesign

Empathize — Define — Ideate — Prototype — Test. This project spanned the entire double-diamond: choosing a government website, conducting research, and redesigning the site.

    Research-based Design Iterations:

    The initial usability test revealed several low-effort, high-value adjustments. The primary areas of focus included:

  • Moving the NASA Logo/Home button to the top left.
  • Move the Explore search field to the right and name it Explore Topics
  • Provide more user-friendly options for the drop-down menu items
  • Create a Podcast page that shows not only the series names, but also lists the episodes within each series
  • Reduce the number of images on the Home page to eliminate scrolling.

    • Although a more concise search algorithm is highly recommended, this that is a large effort and may require metadate updates for all content which is beyond the scope of this project.

    In a follow-up 5-second Test, universal feedback recommended moving the News & Events link to the footer.

    NASA Website Initial State

    The long scrolling home page with multiple navigation options left users confused about where to go and how to return. It's a complicated navigation web which could be streamlined and simplified through more organized drop-down menus.

    The Final Solution A site like NASA with its vast collection of images, research material and news articles would take many rounds of iteration, but could easily benefit from streamlining the home page to reduce scrolling and navigation options that are currently confusing to users. Some of the most impactful and immediate changes would be to remove some of the sub-menu options and clean up the overal alignment. I would like to offer a note of respect goes out to NASA for their impressive baseline, including their well-defined Site Map, documented accessibility standards, and purposeful color palette.
